FACILITIES AND EQUIPMENT

ARM GUARD



Description:

A guard that protects the arm from being hit by the bowstring


CHEST GUARD



Description:

Plastic or leather, to keep clothing out of the way and to protect against a bowstring at release from injuring the body


FINGER TAB



Description:

A flat piece of leather worn as a guard to protect the finger when the arrow releases


TARGET FACE



Description:

A special paper made target which has a 10 ring for scoring.


QUIVER



Description:

A container for holding arrows, usually worn around the waist


SLING



Description:

A string that secure the bows during releasing of shoot. It wear by the archer around his wrist and tie on the other side of string.


ARROW



Description:

Arrows have a maximum diameter of 9.3 millimeters, although, for faster flight and less wind drift, most are as small as 5.5 millimeters. Each arrow must be marked with the competitor's name or initials, while archers use distinctive colors for the nocks and vanes to distinguish their arrows.


TARGET BUTT



Description:

It is material made of compact dried grass which where the arrows thrust during shooting.


BOW STAND



Description:

A metal stand where the bow place when it is not in use.


SIGHT



Description:

A mechanical device placed on the bow to help the archer aim; also called a "bow sight".


HAND GRIP OR HANDLE


STABILIZER



Description:

A weight mounted on the bow to stabilize it during and after a shot.


BOW STRING



Description:

The string of a bow. Most strings are made of high-tech polyethylene fibers, which are stronger than steel.


CLICKER



Description:

It is a soft plastic and metal tool attached above the arrow rest. Signal for the Archer when to release the string when it is click.


LIMBS



Description:

Part of the bow which bends when the bow is tended and where the string attached


ARROW REST



Description:

A piece material on the bow used to support the arrow.


ARROW PARTS

1

Nock

The attachment on the rear end of an arrow that holds it in place on the bowstring.


2

Shaft

A long thin body of the arrow which is usually carbon or aluminum made. Length will depend on the arm length of an archer.


3

Fletch

The real or artificial feathers at the back of an arrow designed when the arrow release.


4

Point

A metal tool that looks like a bullet that thrust into target butt during shooting.


ASSISTIVE DEVICES

RELEASE BRACE/AID



Description:

Release braces hold a bowstring in the drawn position without the need for a hand.


WHEELCHAIR



Description:

Any type is acceptable according the meaning of wheelchair. Chair must not exceed 1.25 m in length.


STOOL



Description:

A chair of any type conforming to the meaning of the word. Space at the shooting line will not exceed 60x80 cm.


BLOCKS



Description:

Possible for Archers with different length of legs. Only one foot.


PERMITTED BODY SUPPORT



Description:

The strapping cannot be more than 2 inches (5 cm) wide at any point)


BOW BANDAGE



Description:

Archers with a bow arm disability may use the bow strapped or tied to the hand.


MOUTH TAB



Description:

For archers who can only use one arm, a mouth tab lets them draw the bowstring with their mouth.


ELBOW STRAP



Description:

Release braces hold a bowstring in the drawn position without the need for a hand.

THE ARCHER



Archery requires its own dress tips. Archery dressing tips are functional. When dressing for archery, the obvious and very essential tip is to avoid any shirt that at all restricts arm movement. Consider protective gear.


I don’t want to be considered as a disabled person who can take part in archery. I want to be considered as an Archer who happens to be disabled. - FITA Coaching Manual
