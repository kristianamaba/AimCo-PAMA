Introduction



Para archery is the sport for athletes who have a physical or visual disability. Targets, competition style, and match play are all the same as they are for target archery and indoor archery.


Para-archery was the first sport where wheelchair athletes had organized competition and was one of the original Paralympic sports. It is additionally one of the sports with the foremost likenesses to its able-bodied partner. In this way, competitors are able to compete in both the Olympics and Paralympics in the event that they meet the capability standards.


Para-archery is open to competitors with physical impedances who may shoot with assistive gadgets permitted beneath classification rules. It incorporates particular competition categories for competitors with certain classifications


To level the playing field, para-archers often use assistive devices such as personalized draw or release aids, mouth tabs, or wheelchairs. Athletes who are classified will participate in target archery events alongside able-bodied athletes while using their assistive devices.


Archery has been a part of the Paralympic movement since the first edition, and has appeared on the schedule of every Paralympic Games since then.


Every two years, World Archery holds a para-archery world championship known as the World Archery Para Championships. The International Paralympic Committee hosted the event for the first time in 1998, and ownership was passed to World Archery in 2009.


Para archers have a global ranking.




History


The sport of archery has a long background in the Paralympic movement.


For people with disabilities, archery is a very open sport. In the 1940s, Dr Ludwig Guttmann at Stoke Mandeville Hospital used it as a therapeutic activity for disabled veterans.


The first Paralympic archery competition was held at the hospital in 1948, with 16 competitors. Guttmann is widely recognized as the founder of the Paralympic movement. The sport was held once a year, and a Dutch team competed in 1952, laying the groundwork for an international event and serving as a forerunner to the Paralympic Games.


Archery debuted at the 1960 Paralympic Games in Rome and has been on the calendar ever since. The International Paralympic Committee administered international para-archery until 2009, when World Archery took over control of the rules, promotion, and regulation of the world championships.




SAFETY IN ARCHERY


Safety in Archery is straight forward and built around common-sense principles. The fundamentals are:


  • Ensure that indoor halls and outdoor venue are conform to health and safety regulations on target positioning.

  • Never allow people to stand ahead of the shooting line.

  • Shooters must shoot in one direction only at the designated target.

  • Shooters can only shoot when permitted.

  • Only allow people always handle bows correctly and responsibly.

  • Always store bows and arrows securely.

  • Always check the equipment if they are in a good condition before shooting.
